What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyberattack where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal information.

Common Goals of Phishing

  • Steal Login Credentials
  • Financial Fraud
  • Identity Theft
  • Install Malware

Recognizing Phishing Emails

Warning Signs

  • Urgent or threatening language
  • Suspicious sender address
  • Spelling and grammar mistakes
  • Unexpected attachments
  • Fake links

🚨 Example Phishing Email

Subject: Your Account Will Be Suspended!

Dear User,
Click immediately to verify your account or it will be permanently locked.

Fake Websites

How Fake Websites Trick Users

  • Copy real website designs
  • Use look-alike URLs
  • Request login credentials
  • Display fake security badges

Real: www.amazon.com

Fake: www.amaz0n-security.com

Social Engineering Tactics

  • Pretexting: Pretending to be a trusted person.
  • Baiting: Offering rewards to trick victims.
  • Scareware: Fake security warnings.
  • Spear Phishing: Targeting specific individuals.

Real-World Example

In a famous phishing attack, employees received emails appearing to be from company executives requesting urgent payments. The attackers successfully stole millions of dollars through fraudulent wire transfers.

Best Practices & Prevention Tips

  • Verify sender email addresses.
  • Never click suspicious links.
  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
  • Keep software updated.
  • Use strong, unique passwords.
  • Report suspicious emails.
